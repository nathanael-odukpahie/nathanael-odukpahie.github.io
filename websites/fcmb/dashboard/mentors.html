Chizoba Michelle Okpala is the Managing Director and Head Mixologist of Elles Icebox, Nigeria’s foremost event support service companies with cocktails and drinks as its specialty. She has a Bachelor of Arts degree in English Language from the University of Lagos and ample experience as a Human Resource Executive at multinational supply chain organization, DHL.

Chizoba got her eureka moment to start her own business after attending an event with poor waiter service. Driven by the need to correct the unprofessionalism that prevailed in the cocktail and drinks business, she setup Elles Ice box in 2008 after undergoing several trainings to perfect the art of mixology. In January 2009, Chizoba set the ship sailing as she catered at her first event which was a huge success.

Building Elles Icebox into a viable business, Chizoba has successfully hosted over 1000 events and has expanded Elles Icebox exponentially to over 10 mobile bars and about 30 employees. Having worked with reputable organizations and clients like Oando Plc, Sahara Energy, First Bank Mortgages, Cross Rivers State Government, Maersk Line, Stat Oil, Standard Chartered Bank, Pastor Ituah Ighodalo, Femi Otedola, MD, Standard Chartered Bank Mrs Bola Adesola and many more, Chizoba has in such a short time distinguished herself in the cocktail and drink business. This has placed Elles Ice Box as the top-of-mind source for cocktails and drinks around Nigeria.

To further strengthen the Elles Icebox brand, Chizoba has formed formidable partnerships with innovative and advancing brands like ShoMya, Sweetkiwi and L'espace where pop up bars have been located. She also writes a weekly column on leading online magazine Bella Naija. The mixologist has also published the 1st cocktail recipe book out of Nigeria ‘The Elles Icebox Cocktail Guide’. In this book she shares some signature cocktail mixes and classics that are loved all over the world. She also wishes to share with budding entrepreneurs achieving dreams in possible and she wishes to empower others through her training school located in Ikeja. For Chizoba, Elles Icebox is more than just cocktails and drinks but also a way of giving back to the society by empowering others with interest in the cocktail and drinks business.

When Chizoba is not busy running the affairs of Elles Icebox, she loves reading.

BUKOLA SEUN-ARIJE

BUKOLA SEUN-ARIJE
BUKOLA Seun-Arije is the Founder and Artistic Director at MiCH & BARJ, a Nigerian fashion brand. Organically growing her business and going through every step in making this a globally successful and sustainable enterprise, with a keen eye and passion for creativity, Bukola designs some of her textiles and garments and other fashion products.

Prior to starting her company / business, Bukola worked in Customer Service and later in Human Resources Management at a global Telecommunications company. She had a brief stint consulting at a Human Resource Firm in Lagos, Nigeria.

Deriving from her unwavering commitment to helping Women, Children and the Youth in general, Bukola has and continues to serve as Volunteer, Counselor, Coach / Teacher, and Partner at Nonprofit organisations in Nigeria and in the United States some of which are “Meals on Wheels”, an organisation that delivers meals to senior individuals at home who are unable to purchase or prepare their own meals, “Lydia’s Heart Initiative / Dabira Woman” an organisation that really does empower girls and their mothers with skills and the ongoing support to better their lives while also providing counseling and support for women who are abused, “Teen Guide Africa”, a Teens / Youth Club that teaches Life Skills / Education, Coaching and support for emotional well-being and “Youth for Christ International” in Nigeria.

Bukola has attended professional development programmes such as the YTP Business Management Programme at Witts Business School in South Africa and WIMBIZ Programmes such as the WIMCAP and currently WIMBOARD Training among others.

Outside of work, Bukola likes to read, particularly enjoys a good novel, likes to cook and eat out, sampling new restaurants and acquiring fresh culinary experiences, likes to travel and definitely wants to travel more. She has a B.A. in English and Literature from University of Ado Ekiti and a Diploma in Human Resource Management from Robert Gordon University, Scotland.

ADERONKE ADERINOYE

ADERONKE ADERINOYE
Aderonke Aderinoye is the Founder and CEO of Agrihub Nigeria. Agrihub has, in the last 2 years alone, trained over 600 individuals in various capacities across the agribusiness value chain from processing to meet global standards to improving efficiency in primary production with over 75% of its participants being women and youths. The organization also provides business support services for national and international organizations (like IFDC2SCALE project, Helen Keller International, Notore PCTIJ training Academy, COLEACP) directed at empowering farmers with the aim of increasing their livelihood and standard of living. As a speaker she has spoken on various platforms including the Main Festival Lagos, Development Dialogue, Lagos, LEAP Africa, British Council Enterprise Summit, Ghana and many more.

Ms. Aderinoye is a Nigerian innovator and has distinguished herself as a passionate catalyst for change. Her major focus is women and youth empowerment specifically developing strategies to reduce poverty, unemployment, boost agricultural development and ensure Food security. She was named as a BBC African Woman to Know in 2016 and featured on France 24, Arise TV and the British Council. She is an Alumna of the US Consulate International Visitors Leadership Program (IVLP) Farm to Table 2013.

Even though she is trained as a Biochemist, she has always had a love for agriculture and promoting the opportunities available within the sector in Africa. As a young person in agriculture herself, her goal is to create pathways to ease other young people into profitable agricultural ventures.
